About Me

I am a Data Analyst working at the intersection of finance, operations and AI for PE-backed software companies.

My work sits where messy spreadsheets, SQL queries and Power BI dashboards meet real questions about growth, profitability and churn. I standardize reporting, build KPI dashboards and automate recurring work so leadership teams can trust the numbers they see each month.

I started on the M&A side, doing hands-on research on software companies‚Äîcleaning, standardizing and expanding datasets so investment teams had something reliable to work with. That early work taught me the value of clean data and consistent logic: if the foundation is wrong, every dashboard and model built on top of it is misleading.

Over time I moved into operations analytics‚Äîdesigning and maintaining Power BI dashboards that track financial performance and key KPIs across software businesses. I spend a lot of time turning scattered Excel files and exports into a single view that leaders can trust for monthly reviews, board reporting and long term planning.

Typical Projects I Work On:

  • Building financial and KPI dashboards in Power BI using data from accounting systems, CRM tools and custom spreadsheets
  • Analyzing trends in revenue, opex and attrition so teams can see where they are growing, where they are leaking and where to focus
  • Working with finance and operations to standardize metrics and definitions so everyone is speaking the same language in meetings
  • Automating recurring reports and refresh schedules so updates run with minimal manual work
  • Using Python and AI tools to scrape, clean and enrich external data for M&A, competitor tracking and market research

I enjoy the "unseen" part of analytics‚Äîcleaning columns, fixing joins, naming measures clearly and designing layouts that non-technical users can read without training. A good dashboard, in my view, is one where an executive can open it for the first time and immediately understand what is going well, what is not and what needs attention.

Recently I have also been leaning into AI-assisted workflows‚Äîusing large language models to document logic, generate draft calculations, create prompt-based QA checks on data and speed up repetitive tasks. I do not see AI as a replacement for analysis; it is a way to remove friction so more time goes into understanding the business instead of fighting with files.
